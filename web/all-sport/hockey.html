Hockey Information

Hockey is a term used to denote a family of various types of both summer and winter team sports which originated on either an outdoor field, sheet of ice, or dry floor such as in a gymnasium. While these sports vary in specific rules, numbers of players, apparel, and playing surface, they share broad characteristics of two opposing teams using sticks to propel a ball or disk into a goal.

MEASUREMENT

RULES

Some important rules for Hockey include:

Offsides: Offensive players can't enter the offensive zone before the puck does.

Icing: A player shoots the puck from behind the red line, and it crosses the blue line without touching a stick.

Fighting: Fighting is a major penalty, and players who start or instigate a fight receive a minor penalty.

Broken stick: Players must drop their stick immediately if it breaks and can't continue playing with it.

Scoring: Goals can only be scored through field goals, penalty corners, or penalty strokes.

Fouls: Fouls result in a free hit or penalty corner for the non-offending side.

Checking: A legal check can stop an attack or keep the puck in the offensive zone.

Penalty killing: An important part of the game, as teams with poor penalty kills can lose a lot of ground quickly.

Stance: An upright skating style can make it difficult to stickhandle the puck.

Playing time: A hockey game is two 35-minute halves, and each team gets a 60-second time-out at half-time.

NSF BODY

HOCKEY INDIA (HI)


Hockey India (HI) is the governing body for field hockey in India. It is responsible for promoting the sport, organizing competitions, and managing the national teams. Hockey India is affiliated with the International Hockey Federation (FIH) and works to develop the game at all levels across the country.
