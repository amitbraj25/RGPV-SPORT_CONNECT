Badminton Information

Badminton is a racquet sport played using racquets to hit a shuttlecock across a net. Although it may be played with larger teams, the most common forms of the game are "singles" (with one player per side) and "doubles" (with two players per side). Badminton is often played as a casual outdoor activity in a yard or on a beach; formal games are played on a rectangular indoor court. Points are scored by striking the shuttlecock with the racquet and landing it within the other team's half of the court.

MEASUREMENT

RULES

Serving - The serve must be hit diagonally across the court, below the waist, and without touching the lines. An incorrect serve results in a fault or a lost point.

Scoring - A player scores a point for each rally won. Matches are best of three games to 21 points, with games won by two clear points. At 29-29, a sudden death point is played.

Faults - A fault occurs if the shuttlecock lands out of bounds, doesn't go over the net, or is hit twice by the same player or two players on the same team. The opposing team scores a point.

Court boundaries - The singles court is narrower, with the wide, outside "tramlines" out of bounds, unlike doubles.

Grip - A correct grip provides better control and helps direct the shuttlecock precisely.

Changing ends - Players change ends at the end of the first game, and again at the end of the second game if a third game is required. In the third game, players change ends when one side reaches 11 points.

NSF BODY

Badminton Association of India (BAI)


The Badminton Association of India (BAI) is the governing body for badminton in India. Established in 1934, BAI is responsible for organizing national-level badminton tournaments, promoting the sport across the country, and selecting players for international competitions. BAI is affiliated with the Badminton World Federation (BWF) and the Badminton Asia Confederation (BAC), working in line with these organizations to develop and improve badminton at all levels.
