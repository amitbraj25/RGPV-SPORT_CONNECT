Weightlifting Information


Weightlifting or weight lifting generally refers to physical exercises and sports in which people lift weights, often in the form of (dumbbells, barbells or machines). People engage in weightlifting for a variety of different reasons. These can include: developing physical strength; promoting health and fitness; competing in weightlifting sports; and developing a muscular and aesthetic physique.

MEASUREMENT

RULES

Warm up: Warming up for 5â€“10 minutes before lifting weights helps prepare your muscles and joints, and reduces the risk of injury.

Maintain proper form: Proper form is more important than the amount of weight you use.

Move slowly: Moving slowly and in a controlled manner helps you isolate the muscles you want to work.

Rest: Rest for about a minute between each exercise.

Don't overdo: Completing one set of exercises to the point of fatigue is usually enough.

Don't ignore pain: If an exercise causes pain, stop and try again in a few days or with less weight.

Wear shoes: Wear shoes that protect your feet and provide good traction.

Keep equipment clean: Keeping equipment clean prevents the spread of germs and reduces slipping hazards.

NSF BODY

Indian Weightlifting Federation (IWLF)


The Indian Weightlifting Federation (IWLF) is the national governing body for weightlifting in India. It is responsible for promoting and developing weightlifting at various levels, organizing national competitions, and selecting athletes for international events. The IWLF aims to enhance the visibility of weightlifting and support the growth of the sport across the country.
