Cricket Information


Cricket is a bat-and-ball game played between two teams of eleven players on a field at the center of which is a 22-yard (20-metre) pitch with a wicket at each end, each comprising two bails balanced on three stumps.

The length of a cricket pitch is 22 yards (20.12 meters). The width of the pitch is 10 feet (3.05 meters). The boundary lengths can vary but usually range between 65 yards (59.4 meters) and 90 yards (82.3 meters).

MEASUREMENT

RULES

Teams: There are two teams of 11 players each.

Pitch: The pitch has two ends, each with three stumps called a wicket.

Turns: Each team takes turns batting and bowling.

Scoring: The batting team tries to score runs by hitting the ball and running to the other end of the pitch, or by hitting the ball over the boundary.

Fielding: The fielding team tries to get the batters out by hitting the wickets, catching a shot, or using LBW.

Winning: The team with the most runs at the end of the match wins.

Dismissal: There are ten ways a batsman can be dismissed, and each dismissal has its own rules.

Hitting the ball twice: A batter cannot hit the ball twice unless the fielder first touches it.

Innings: The captain of the batting side can declare an innings closed at any time during the innings.

Forfeiture: The captain can forfeit either of their side's innings before the start of that innings.

NSF BODY

Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI)


The Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI) is the national governing body for cricket in India. It is responsible for organizing domestic competitions, managing the Indian national team, and representing Indian cricket at the international level. The BCCI plays a crucial role in promoting cricket in India and is affiliated with the International Cricket Council (ICC).
