Chess Information

Chess is an abstract strategy game that involves no hidden information and no elements of chance. It is played on a chessboard with 64 squares arranged in an 8×8 grid. The players, referred to as "White" and "Black", each control sixteen pieces: one king, one queen, two rooks, two bishops, two knights, and eight pawns. White moves first, followed by Black. The game is typically won by checkmating the opponent's king, i.e. threatening it with inescapable capture. There are several ways a game can end in a draw.

MEASUREMENT

RULES

Some important rules for Chess include:

Objective: The goal is to checkmate the opponent’s king, putting it in a position where it cannot escape capture.


Piece Movement: Each piece has unique movement rules:
King: 1 square in any direction
Queen: Any number of squares in any direction
Rook: Horizontally or vertically
Bishop: Diagonally
Knight: L-shape, can jump over pieces
Pawn: Forward 1 square, captures diagonally

Check and Checkmate: When in check, a player must move to protect the king. Failure to escape leads to checkmate.

Castling: A special move that allows the king and a rook to move simultaneously, under certain conditions (neither has moved, no check, no pieces in between).

Pawn Promotion: When a pawn reaches the opposite side of the board, it can be promoted to any piece (except a king).

En Passant: A pawn can capture an opponent's pawn if it moves two squares forward and lands beside it, as if it had only moved one square.

Stalemate: If a player has no legal moves and is not in check, the game ends in a draw (stalemate).

Draw Conditions: The game can end in a draw by stalemate, threefold repetition, the fifty-move rule, insufficient material, or mutual agreement.

NSF BODY

All India Chess Federation (AICF)


The All India Chess Federation (AICF) is the national governing body for chess in India. It promotes and regulates chess activities across the country, organizes national tournaments, selects players for international events, and ensures the sport is played according to international standards. AICF is affiliated with FIDE, the International Chess Federation.
