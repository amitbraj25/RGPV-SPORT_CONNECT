Volley Ball Information


Volleyball is a team sport in which two teams of six players are separated by a net. Each team tries to score points by grounding a ball on the other team's court under organized rules. It has been a part of the official program of the Summer Olympic Games since Tokyo 1964. Beach volleyball was introduced to the programme at the Atlanta 1996 Summer Olympics. The adapted version of volleyball at the Summer Paralympic Games is sitting volleyball.

MEASUREMENT

RULES

Players: There are six players per team, three in the front row and three in the back row.

Serving: The serve starts each rally and can be underhand or overhand. A good serve puts the opposing team at a disadvantage.

Scoring: The winning team of each rally scores a point. This is called rally-point scoring.

Ball handling: It's illegal to catch, hold, or throw the ball.

Ball placement: A ball that hits a boundary line is in, but a ball that hits the floor outside the court, the net, or the referee stand is out.

Passing: Passing is the first contact with the ball after a serve. The goal is to get the ball to the setter in a controlled way.

Blocking: A player can't block or attack a serve from inside the 10-foot line.

Switching: After the serve, front-line players can switch positions at the net.

Double faults: If two opponents touch the ball at the same time, it's a double fault and the rally is replayed.

NSF BODY

Volleyball Federation of India (VFI)


The Volleyball Federation of India (VFI) is the national governing body for volleyball in India. It is responsible for promoting and developing volleyball at various levels, organizing national competitions, and selecting athletes for international events. The VFI aims to enhance the visibility of volleyball and support the growth of the sport across the country.
