Gymnastics Information


Gymnastics is a type of sport that includes physical exercises requiring balance, strength, flexibility, agility, coordination, artistry and endurance. The movements involved in gymnastics contribute to the development of the arms, legs, shoulders, back, chest, and abdominal muscle groups. Gymnastics evolved from exercises used by the ancient Greeks that included skills for mounting and dismounting a horse, and from circus performance skills.

RULES

Some important rules for Gymnastics include:

Safety: Gymnasts should warm up, stretch, and practice on padded floors. They should also wear gymnastic clothes that don't get caught on equipment and avoid jewelry and gum.

Competition: Gymnasts compete in qualifications to determine who advances to the finals. In women's gymnastics, the all-around champion is the gymnast with the highest combined score across all four pieces of apparatus.

Banned moves: There are banned moves in both men's and women's gymnastics. For example, in women's gymnastics, vaults with sideward takeoffs or landings are not allowed.

Composure: Gymnasts should keep their emotions in check, whether they win or lose.

Golden Rules: The 10 Golden Rules of Gymnastics include respecting others, acting with integrity, and balancing gymnastics with other goals.

NSF BODY

Gymnastics Federation of India (GFI)


The Gymnastics Federation of India (GFI) is the national governing body for the sport of gymnastics in India. It organizes competitions, manages athlete development programs, and represents Indian gymnastics internationally. The GFI is affiliated with the International Gymnastics Federation (FIG).
