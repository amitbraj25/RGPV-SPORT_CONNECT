Kabaddi Information


Kabaddi is a contact team sport played between two teams of seven players, originating in ancient India. The objective of the game is for a single player on offence, referred to as a "raider", to run into the opposing team's half of the court, touch out as many of their players as possible, and return to their own half of the court, all without being tackled by the defenders in 30 seconds. Points are scored for each player tagged by the raider, while the opposing team earns a point for stopping the raider. Players are taken out of the game if they are touched or tackled, but return to the game after each point scored by their team from a tag or tackle.

MEASUREMENT

RULES

The Field: The mat is split in half by a mid line, with two parallel lines in each half. The baulk line is 3.75 meters from the mid line, and the bonus line is 1 meter behind the baulk line.

The Objective: The raider's goal is to tag as many opposing players as possible without getting caught while raiding into the other team's court. The defenders try to stop the raider from returning to their own half.

Scoring: If the raider is successfully tackled, the opposing team earns a point. If a team gets all seven players on the opposing team out, they earn two additional points.

Reviving Players: All tagged players are taken out of the game, but one is "revived" for each point a team scores from a subsequent tag or tackle.

Golden Raid: If the first raiding player scores even a single point, their team wins the match.

Out of Bounds: Players who step out of the boundary are out.

Holding: If a defender who has touched the ground outside the boundary holds a raider, the raider is not out.

NSF BODY

Amateur Kabaddi Federation of India (AKFI)


The Amateur Kabaddi Federation of India (AKFI) is the national governing body for Kabaddi in India. It is responsible for promoting and developing Kabaddi at various levels, organizing national competitions, and selecting athletes for international events. The AKFI aims to enhance the visibility of Kabaddi and support the growth of the sport across the country.
