Athletics Information


Athletics is a term encompassing the human competitive sports and games requiring physical skill, and the systems of training that prepare athletes for competitive performance. Athletic sports or contests are competitions which are primarily based on human physical competition, demanding the qualities of stamina, fitness, and skill.

MEASUREMENT

RULES

Stay in your lane: In track and field, runners must stay in their assigned lanes from start to finish.

Start behind the line: Runners must start behind the starting line.

Don't jump the gun: A false start can result in disqualification.

Wear protective gear: Protective gear like shin pads, helmets, elbow guards, and mouth guards can help prevent injuries.

Rest and recover: It's important to take time to rest and recover after training or a workout.

Don't play when injured: Playing when injured can delay healing and be dangerous.

Wear sneakers or athletic shoes: Runners must wear sneakers or athletic shoes.

No hats or head coverings: Hats or head coverings are generally not allowed during competition, except for medical reasons.

Relay team members wear identical shirts: All relay team members should wear identical shirts or singlets.

Implement specifications: All implements used in Olympic competition must meet the specifications set by the IAAF.

NSF BODY

Athletics Federation of India (AFI)


The Athletics Federation of India (AFI) is the governing body for the sport of athletics in India. It organizes national competitions, promotes the sport, and ensures adherence to international standards.
