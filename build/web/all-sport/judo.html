Judo Information


Judo is an unarmed modern Japanese martial art, combat sport, Olympic sport (since 1964), and the most prominent form of jacket wrestling competed internationally. Judo was created in 1882 by Kanō Jigorō as an eclectic martial art, distinguishing itself from its predecessors instead of kata alongside its removal of striking and weapon training elements.[3][4][6] Judo rose to prominence for its dominance over established jujutsu schools in tournaments hosted by the Tokyo Metropolitan Police Department , resulting in its adoption as the department's primary martial art.A judo practitioner is called a "judoka" , and the judo uniform is called "judogi" .

MEASUREMENT

RULES

Safety: It's not allowed to intentionally injure an opponent, punch, kick, or touch their face. You can't attack joints other than the elbow, or perform head dives. You also can't wear hard or metallic objects, such as wedding rings or hair ties with metal parts.

Grips: You can't deliberately avoid gripping, and you can't hold a non-normal grip for too long without attacking. A normal grip is when your right hand grips your opponent's left jacket side, and your left hand grips their right jacket side.

Starting Positions: You can choose to start in a standing position (tachi-waza) or a kneeling or sitting position (ne-waza). If an athlete starts in ne-waza due to a disability, their opponent must also start in ne-waza.

Match Length: The length of a match depends on the age category and level of competition. For example, senior national and international matches are four minutes long.

Scoring: If the match goes the full length, the winner is the judoka with the most waza-aris. If there's a tie, the winner is decided by "golden score", which is like sudden-death overtime.

NSF BODY

Judo Federation of India (JFI)


The Judo Federation of India (JFI) is the national governing body for Judo in India. It is responsible for promoting the sport at various levels, organizing national competitions, and selecting athletes for international events. The JFI works to develop Judo in the country and is affiliated with the International Judo Federation (IJF).
