Kho-Kho Information


Kho kho is a traditional South Asian sport that dates to ancient India.It is the second-most popular traditional tag game in the Indian subcontinent after kabaddi. Kho kho is played on a rectangular court with a central lane connecting two poles which are at either end of the court. During the game, nine players from the chasing team (attacking team) are on the field, with eight of them sitting (crouched) in the central lane, while three runners from the defending team run around the court and try to avoid being touched.[2] Each sitting player on the chasing team faces the opposite half of the field that their adjacent teammates are facing.

MEASUREMENT

RULES

Teams: There are two teams, each with 12 players, though at least six are required.

Players: The chasers line up on the middle line, facing in alternating directions, and can only chase on their side of the field. Defenders enter the field in groups of three and can run anywhere on the field.

Objective: The chasers try to tag the defenders and get them out as quickly as possible.

Winning: The team that gets the defenders out the fastest wins.

Play: A standard match has two innings, each lasting nine minutes, with a break between innings.

Ground: The field is marked with a grid of lines that divides it into two equal parts.

Dress Code: Players wear sports shoes, and men and boys wear shorts and a vest or T-shirt, while women and girls wear a shirt or T-shirt.

Toss: A toss decides which team runs and which chases.

Passing: If a chaser can't get out three runners in a row, they must pass the kho to a sitting chaser.

NSF BODY

Kho Kho Federation of India (KKFI)


The Kho Kho Federation of India (KKFI) is the national governing body for Kho-Kho in India. It is responsible for promoting and developing Kho-Kho at various levels, organizing national competitions, and selecting athletes for international events. The KKFI aims to enhance the visibility of Kho-Kho and support the growth of the sport across the country.
