Takewondo Information


Taekwondo is a Korean martial art and combat sport involving punching and kicking techniques. The word Taekwondo can be translated as tae ("strike with foot"), kwon ("strike with hand"), and do ("the art or way"). In addition to its five tenets of courtesy, integrity, perseverance, self-control and indomitable spirit, the sport requires three physical skills: poomsae, kyorugi and gyeokpa.

MEASUREMENT

RULES

Blows below the head: These are awarded points.

Knockdown: A knockdown is worth three points.

Tenets: The five key tenets of taekwondo are courtesy, integrity, perseverance, self-control, and indomitable spirit.

Sparring match length: A match consists of three rounds of two minutes each, with one-minute breaks between rounds.

Prohibited acts: These include:

  • Stepping outside the boundary line.
  • Pushing, grabbing, or holding an opponent.
  • Feigning injury.
  • Turning your back towards the opponent.
  • Attacking the opponent's head with anything but your feet.
  • Throwing an opponent.
  • Deliberately pulling the opponent down to the ground.

Etiquette: Some examples of etiquette include:

  • Bowing when entering and exiting.
  • Turning away to straighten your uniform.
  • Not playing with the national flag.
  • Listening when asked to do something.
  • Bowing to high-ranking belts.

NSF BODY

Taekwondo Federation of India (TFI)


The Taekwondo Federation of India (TFI) is the national governing body for taekwondo in India. It is responsible for promoting and developing the sport at various levels, organizing national competitions, and selecting athletes for international events. The TFI aims to enhance the visibility of taekwondo and support the growth of the sport across the country.
