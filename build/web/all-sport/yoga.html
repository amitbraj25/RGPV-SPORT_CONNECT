Yoga Information


yoga is a group of physical, mental, and spiritual practices or disciplines practiced in Hinduism, Buddhism, and Jainism, aimed at controlling body and mind and attaining a soteriological goal as specified by a specific tradition.

RULES

Breathing: Breathing techniques, known as pranayama, are a vital part of yoga. They can help you control your breathing and use your lungs more fully.

Breath awareness: Breath awareness is at the core of yoga and can help settle your mind, calm your nervous system, and relax your body.

Meditation: A calm and steady mind is important for keeping your body in good condition. Positive thinking can help purify your mind and experience inner peace.

Practice on an empty stomach: It's generally recommended to wait 1â€“2 hours after eating before practicing yoga.

Wear comfortable clothes: Yoga requires a lot of stretching and movement, so comfortable clothing is important to help you move easily.

Asanas: Asanas, or poses, and the sequence of poses are important for creating a response in your body, such as stimulating, energizing, or calming it.

Pratyahara: This Sanskrit term translates to "withdrawal of the senses" and is considered a crucial first step before more difficult forms of concentration and meditation.

Yoga Federation Of India (YFI)


The Yoga Federation of India (YFI) is the national governing body for yoga in India. It promotes yoga practices, organizes national competitions, and works to spread awareness about the health benefits of yoga. The YFI also aims to foster a community of yoga practitioners and instructors across the country.
