Boxing Information


Boxing is a combat sport and martial art. Taking place in a boxing ring, it involves two people – usually wearing protective equipment, such as protective gloves, hand wraps, and mouthguards – throwing punches at each other for a predetermined amount of time.

MEASUREMENT

RULES

No hitting on the break: Boxers can't strike their opponent when the bell sounds to end a round .

Ring control: The fighter who controls the ring usually wins the fight.

Weight classes: Boxers must fight opponents in the same weight class.

Gloves: In professional fights, gloves must weigh between 8-10 ounces.

No wrestling: Boxers can't wrestle or hug their opponent.

Knocked down: A boxer who is knocked down must get up on their own.

No hitting below the belt: Boxers can't hit below their opponent's waist.

Keep hands up: Boxers should keep their hands close to their face to block punches.

Effective aggression: Boxers should take the lead and throw destructive punches.

Knockdowns are scored heavily: A knockdown is a clear sign of dominance in a round.

NSF BODY

Boxing Federation of India (BFI)


The Boxing Federation of India (BFI) is the national governing body for the sport of boxing in India. It organizes national tournaments, selects athletes for international competitions, and promotes the sport at all levels. BFI is affiliated with the International Boxing Association (AIBA) and works closely with other organizations to ensure the sport is developed according to international standards.
