Squash-Racket Information


Squash, sometimes called squash rackets, is a racket-and-ball sport played by two (singles) or four players (doubles) in a four-walled court with a small, hollow, rubber ball. The players alternate in striking the ball with their rackets onto the playable surfaces of the four walls of the court. The objective of the game is to hit the ball in such a way that the opponent is not able to play a valid return. There are about 20 million people who play squash regularly world-wide in over 185 countries. The governing body of squash, the World Squash Federation (WSF), is recognized by the International Olympic Committee (IOC), and the sport will be included in the Olympic Games, starting with the 2028 Summer Olympics in Los Angeles. The Professional Squash Association (PSA) organizes the pro tour.

MEASUREMENT

RULES

Scoring: Players take turns hitting the ball against the front wall, and the goal is to make your opponent miss their shot. Each game is played to 11 points, and the first player to 11 points wins. If the score reaches 10-all, the game continues until one player leads by two points.

Serving: The server must stand in a service box, hit the ball between the service line and out line, and land it on the opposite side of the court.

Ball movement: The ball must hit the front wall on each shot but can hit other walls before or after. The opponent must hit the ball before it bounces twice.

Movement: Players can move anywhere on the court, but they can't obstruct the other player's movements.

Lets: If there is interference with your play, you can request a "let" to replay the game.

Racket grip: Hold the racket like a paintbrush, with your fingers angled slightly and the grip sitting across your fingers.

NSF BODY

Squash Rackets Federation of India (SRFI)


The Squash Rackets Federation of India (SRFI) is the national governing body for squash in India. It is responsible for promoting and developing squash at various levels, organizing national competitions, and selecting athletes for international events. The SRFI aims to enhance the visibility of squash and support the growth of the sport across the country.
