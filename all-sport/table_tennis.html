Table Tennis Information


Table tennis is a racket sport derived from tennis but distinguished by its playing surface being atop a stationary table, rather than the court on which players stand. Either individually or in teams of two, players take alternating turns returning a light, hollow ball over the table's net onto the opposing half of the court using small rackets until they fail to do so, which results in a point for the opponent. Play is fast, requiring quick reaction and constant attention, and is characterized by an emphasis on spin, which can affect the ball's trajectory more than in other ball sports.

MEASUREMENT

RULES

Serving: The ball must be tossed at least 6 inches straight up and struck on the way down. The ball must bounce once on your side of the table and then once on your opponent's side. If the ball hits the net and goes over, it's a "let," and the serve is replayed. If the ball hits the net and doesn't go over or flies off the table without bouncing on your opponent's side, you lose the point.

Scoring: Games are usually played to 11 points, and matches are the best of any odd-numbered games.

Serving order: Players alternate serving every two points, but if the score reaches 10-10, players change servers every point.

Net: The net should be 6 inches above the playing surface and as close as possible to the playing surface and the supporting posts.

Obstructing the ball: If a player obstructs the ball, they lose the point.

Striking the ball: A player cannot strike the ball twice in a row.

NSF BODY

Table Tennis Federation of India (TTFI)


The Table Tennis Federation of India (TTFI) is the national governing body for table tennis in India. It is responsible for promoting and developing the sport at various levels, organizing national competitions, and selecting athletes for international events. The TTFI aims to enhance the visibility of table tennis and support the growth of the sport across the country.
