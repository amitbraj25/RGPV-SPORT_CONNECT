Wrestling Information


Wrestling is a martial art and combat sport that involves grappling with an opponent and striving to obtain a position of advantage through different throws or techniques, within a given ruleset. Wrestling involves different grappling-type techniques, such as clinch fighting, throws and takedowns, joint locks, pins, and other grappling holds. Many different wrestling techniques have been incorporated into martial arts, combat sports, and military systems.

MEASUREMENT

RULES

Weight classes: Organized weight classes are important to keep wrestlers safe. A lightweight wrestler could be seriously injured in a match against a heavyweight wrestler.

Illegal holds: Dangerous holds are not allowed, and the referee will stop the match and award a point to the opponent. For example, a slam is an illegal hold that involves lifting an opponent to the mat with too much force.

Unsportsmanlike conduct: Unsportsmanlike behavior can result in a point for the opponent. This includes unnecessary roughness, stalling, and using an illegal hold.

Fall or pin: A pin or fall is the ultimate way to win a match and is worth six points. A pin occurs when both shoulders of an opponent are placed on the mat.

Technical fall: A technical fall is awarded when a wrestler has a lead of 15 or more points, and the match is ended.

Reversals: Reversals are important because they help dictate the pace of a match.

Lifting: Lifting is a vital wrestling skill that's used in many moves. Wrestlers should primarily lift with their legs, not their arms.

NSF BODY

Wrestling Federation of India (WFI)


The Wrestling Federation of India (WFI) is the national governing body for wrestling in India. It is responsible for promoting and developing wrestling at various levels, organizing national competitions, and selecting athletes for international events. The WFI aims to enhance the visibility of wrestling and support the growth of the sport across the country.
