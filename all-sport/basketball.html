Basketball Information

Basketball is a team sport in which two teams, most commonly of five players each, opposing one another on a rectangular court, compete with the primary objective of shooting a basketball (approximately 9.4 inches (24 cm) in diameter) through the defender's hoop (a basket 18 inches (46 cm) in diameter mounted 10 feet (3.048 m) high to a backboard at each end of the court), while preventing the opposing team from shooting through their own hoop.

MEASUREMENT

RULES

The ball may be thrown in any direction with one or both hands.

The ball may be batted in any direction with one or both hands (never with a fist).

A player cannot run with the ball; they must throw it from the spot where they catch it, allowing for catching while running at speed.

The ball must be held in or between the hands; the arms or body must not be used for holding it.

No shouldering, holding, pushing, tripping, or striking the person of an opponent is allowed. The first infringement counts as a foul; the second disqualifies the player until the next goal is made.

A foul occurs when striking at the ball with the fist or violating the aforementioned rules.

If either side makes three consecutive fouls, it counts as a goal for the opponents.

A goal is made when the ball is thrown or batted from the ground into the basket and stays there.

When the ball goes out of bounds, it shall be thrown into the field and played by the first person touching it.

The umpire will judge fouls, notifying the referee when three consecutive fouls have been made.

The referee will decide when the ball is in play, in bounds, to which side it belongs, and keep the time.

Each game consists of two fifteen-minute halves, with a five-minute rest between halves.

The side with the most goals at the end of the game is declared the winner. In case of a draw, the game may continue until another goal is made.

NSF BODY

Basketball Federation of India (BFI)


The Basketball Federation of India (BFI) is the governing body for basketball in India. Established in 1950, BFI is responsible for the development of basketball at all levels, organizing national-level tournaments, and selecting players for international competitions. BFI is affiliated with FIBA (Fédération Internationale de Basketball) and works in collaboration with state associations to promote the sport across the country.
