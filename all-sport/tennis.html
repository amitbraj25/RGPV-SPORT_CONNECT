Tennis Information


Tennis is a racket sport that is played either individually against a single opponent (singles) or between two teams of two players each (doubles). Each player uses a tennis racket strung with a cord to strike a hollow rubber ball covered with felt over or around a net and into the opponent's court. The object of the game is to manoeuvre the ball in such a way that the opponent is not able to play a valid return. The player unable to return the ball validly will not gain a point, while the opposite player will.

MEASUREMENT

RULES

Winning a point: To win a point, a player must hit the ball before it bounces twice, or after the first bounce. If the ball bounces twice, the player who hit the last shot wins.

Serving: The serve is one of the most important shots in tennis, and it's the only shot where the player has complete control.

Net: Players can't step over the net to hit the ball.

Ball touching the racket twice: If the ball touches the player's racket twice, the point goes to the opponent.

Ball getting caught in the net: If the ball gets caught in the net, the point goes to the opponent.

Score calling: If you're serving, you should call the score before each point. This lets your opponent know you're ready to serve and helps prevent score disputes.

Respecting other courts: Treat other players' courts as you would want yours to be treated.

Balance: Balance is an important skill in tennis.

NSF BODY

All India Tennis Association (AITA)


The All India Tennis Association (AITA) is the national governing body for tennis in India. It is responsible for promoting and developing tennis at various levels, organizing national competitions, and selecting athletes for international events. The AITA aims to enhance the visibility of tennis and support the growth of the sport across the country.
