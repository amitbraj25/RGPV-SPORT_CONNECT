Softball Information


Softball is a popular variation of baseball, the difference being that it is played with a larger ball, on a smaller field, and with only underhand pitches (where the ball is released while the hand is primarily below the ball) permitted. Softball is played competitively at club levels, the college level, and the professional level. The game was first created in 1887 in Chicago by George Hancock.

MEASUREMENT

RULES

Infield fly rule: A fair fly ball can be caught by an infielder, pitcher, or catcher using ordinary effort.

Pitchers throw underhand: Underhand throwing is essential for softball pitching.

Three outs per inning: The defense needs to get three outs per inning for the game to advance.

Runners must touch bases in order: Runners can't lead off a base or pass another runner ahead of them.

Umpires make decisions: Umpires make decisions about play in a softball game.

Fielding: Good defensive fielding can be the difference between winning and losing.

Pitch count guidelines: Coaches should establish and keep to strict pitch count guidelines to prevent overuse injuries.

Coaches act as umpires: Coaches can pitch to their own team, and a second coach can help field to speed up the game.

NSF BODY

Softball Association of India (SBAI)


The Softball Association of India (SBAI) is the national governing body for softball in India. It is responsible for promoting and developing the sport at various levels, organizing national competitions, and selecting athletes for international events. The SBAI aims to enhance the visibility of softball and support the growth of the sport across the country.
